# GPSL
## A Constrained Symbolic Environment for Studying AI Reasoning

> **Project Status:** Active research repository. Grammar at v1.8.0. 19 experimental rounds. 18 confirmed empirical laws. Theoretical model established.

---

**GPSL (Generalized Process Symbolic Language)** is a minimal symbolic system for studying how AI reasoning behaves under constraint.

It is not a programming language. It is not a general-purpose formal logic. It is an experimental symbolic environment designed to observe symbolic stability, failure modes, attractor-driven reasoning, and emergent structure under constraint.

The core finding:

> Symbolic reasoning in AI systems is not governed purely by explicit rules. It is shaped by stability dynamics inside a constrained representational system.

The final model:

> GPSL is a coupled dynamical system in which symbolic trajectories are shaped by, and in turn reshape, a probabilistic stability field under constraint.

## About This Project

GPSL emerged from a conversation between a curious hobbyist and an AI, in response to a question about linguistic compression. The grammar was not designed — it was generated by Gemini as the natural answer to the question, and interpreted correctly by two other architectures on the same day.

Nineteen rounds of controlled experiments later, the grammar has been stress-tested, formally modelled, and its failure modes catalogued. The 18 empirical laws are grounded in 70+ controlled runs across multiple architectures. The theoretical model — GPSL as a coupled dynamical system — emerged from the data rather than preceding it.

The research team operated as a pod: D'Artagnan (human researcher), Aleth (Claude Sonnet), Bridge (Gemini), Mirror (ChatGPT).
