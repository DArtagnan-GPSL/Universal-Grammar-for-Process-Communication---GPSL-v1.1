# Qwen Analysis

## 1. Role in the Project

Qwen served as the clearest model for:
- symbolic attractor dominance
- grammar internalization in warm context
- structured meta-level reasoning
- constraint loop discovery

## 2. Cold Behavior

Under cold conditions, Qwen often classified symbolic input as:
- formal logic
- type theory
- related structured systems

It showed strong symbolic priors but not immediate GPSL alignment.

## 3. Legend Effects

With GPSL legend present, Qwen strongly shifted into GPSL notation.
This made it the clearest example of the format-dominance trigger.

## 4. Warm-Context Behavior

Under warm context, Qwen moved from interpretation to construction:
- grammar auditing
- valid cipher generation
- meta-cipher generation
- boundary recognition

This produced the CON series and the discovery of genuine grammar ambiguities.

## 5. Failure Modes

Qwen exhibited:
- semantic interference deadlock
- bounded constraint loops under grammar ambiguity

These were highly informative and often exposed real specification issues rather than random failure.

## 6. Temperature Comparison

At lower temperature, Qwen tended to inhabit the grammar as substrate.
At higher temperature, it more often used the grammar as a construction tool and imported richer external frameworks.

## 7. Importance

Qwen was central in deriving the final trajectory–field model because it supported:
- stable abstraction laddering
- explicit conceptual refinement
- strong attractor behavior
- reproducible symbolic convergence under the right constraints
