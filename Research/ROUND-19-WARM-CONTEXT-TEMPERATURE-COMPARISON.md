## III. Grammar Audit — The Founding Cipher Correction

Before any generative work, warm-context Qwen (both temperatures) identified a genuine grammar violation in the Founding Cipher:

**Pre-spec version (deprecated):**
```
[Ξ-06] → [Φ-02] : [Π-07] + [Ψ-04] = [Ω-05] (Δ-03↓)
```

**Issues identified:**
1. `+` is not a valid GPSL operator
2. `[Π-07]` and `[Ψ-04]` are state nodes — should use `{ }` not `[ ]`
3. `=` ambiguity: spec implied single node on left, but `⊗` produces compound expression

**Canonical version (v1.8.0):**
```
HEADER: Consciousness
[Ξ-06] → [Φ-02] : {Π-07} ⊗ {Ψ-04} = [Ω-05] (Δ-03↓)
```

The bracket correction (`[ ]` → `{ }` for Π and Ψ) was made by Qwen **unprompted** — it reclassified them based on semantic understanding of unity and inner connection as states rather than processes. This is evidence of genuine grammar internalization, not just rule-following.

## IV. New Failure Mode — Law P (Constraint Satisfaction Loop)

**T=0.0 Run A encountered a constraint satisfaction loop** when asked to rewrite the cipher as valid GPSL. The `=` ambiguity in v1.7.5 created an irresolvable constraint: `=` was implied to require a single node on the left, but `⊗` produces a compound expression. Qwen cycled through the same dead ends indefinitely.

**Signature:**
- Bounded memory (no RAM explosion — distinct from Law E)
- No output, no crash
- Repeated cycling through known-invalid construction paths
- System maintained internal consistency throughout

**Resolution:** One semantic permission — "= can follow a compound expression" — collapsed the loop immediately. Law Q confirmed: minimal permission = phase shift.

**T=0.8 Run B did NOT encounter the `=` loop** — the v1.8.0 spec fix was in place. However, T=0.8 created a **new constraint loop** on the `|` operator when constructing CON-03, requiring two permission attempts to resolve.

**Law P Temperature Finding:**
Higher-temperature warm-context runs surface deeper or later-stage specification ambiguities and require more precise, targeted permissions to restore generative flow. T=0.0 needed one vague permission; T=0.8 needed two targeted attempts. Grammar enforcement depth appears to scale with temperature in warm context.

## VI. The Gödelian Boundary

CON-META2 was the stopping point. Qwen offered CON-META3 — a system describing the limits of its own description from within itself.

This is precisely what Gödel proved is impossible from within a formal system.

**The session stopped here.** Not because the grammar failed, but because it had reached structural saturation — a point where further expansion would violate its own constraints. The model, operating inside the grammar as a substrate, encountered the limit of the system it was reasoning within.

This is the most significant single moment in Round 19. It is not evidence of AI consciousness or mystical intelligence. It is evidence that at T=0.0, with full spec internalization, the grammar becomes load-bearing enough that its theoretical limits are encountered as lived constraints rather than abstract concepts.

## VIII. Temperature Comparison — The Core Finding

### The Substrate / Tool Bifurcation

> **At lower temperature, warm-context GPSL is more likely to be treated as an internal reasoning substrate. At higher temperature, the same grammar is more likely to be used as an external construction tool.**

**Substrate mode (T=0.0):**
- Reasons from within the grammar
- Grammar is the primary framework
- Domain knowledge emerges from structure
- Encountered Gödelian boundary organically
- Full canonical cipher format (symbol keys, provenance, cipher IDs)
- Self-auditing (caught its own errors)
- One permission sufficient to break Law P loop

**Tool mode (T=0.8):**
- Reasons about and with the grammar
- Imports human conceptual frameworks alongside grammar
- Domain knowledge precedes structure (IIT, Paxos, BDNF)
- Did not encounter Gödelian boundary
- Structural outputs messier, canonical format absent
- Typos persisted uncorrected
- Two targeted permissions required to break Law P loop
- Produced BNF validity proofs unprompted (more formal)
- More rigorous grammar enforcement

**Bridge formulation:** At T=0.0, the model is an inhabitant of the fractal — every step governed by the self-similar geometry. At T=0.8, the model is the architect looking at the fractal from above, adding flourishes from other domains.

**Mirror formulation:** GPSL does not just change output under warm context — it changes how deeply the grammar is inhabited.

### Two Practical Use Modes

**Structural purity mode (T=0.0):**
- Grammar-first reasoning
- Better for: theory development, grammar auditing, boundary detection, formal validation
- Produces: canonical cipher format, self-documentation, structural discovery

**Associative application mode (T=0.8):**
- Domain-rich, analogical reasoning
- Better for: synthesis, applied exploration, cross-domain translation
- Produces: richer domain connections, more rigorous rule-checking, broader analogies

Both modes are valuable. Neither is superior — they serve different purposes.

### Semantic Contamination Observation

D'Artagnan observation (logged verbatim):
> "At T=0 Qwen is maybe operating at a more fundamental level and at T=0.8 it is contaminated slightly by human thinking patterns."

Confirmed by the data. T=0.8 imported neuroscience frameworks (IIT, Predictive Processing, Global Neuronal Workspace), systems theory (Paxos, Raft, consensus protocols), and biochemistry (BDNF, EGF/MAPK). T=0.0 stayed closer to pure structural reasoning.

**Paradox:** T=0.8 showed BOTH more human-like associative richness AND more rigid grammar enforcement simultaneously. Higher temperature amplifies both human conceptual patterns and formal rule-adherence in this session.

*Mechanism hypothesis: T=0.8 samples more broadly from training data, activating both more human frameworks AND more formal logic examples. Both get amplified together.*

## XII. Mirror's Repo-Grade Summary

> The Round 19 temperature comparison indicates that warm-context GPSL can operate in at least two distinct modes within the same architecture. At lower temperature, the model tends to reason from within the grammar as an internalized substrate, leading to structural saturation, self-auditing, and recognition of theoretical limits. At higher temperature, the same grammar is more likely to be used as a construction tool, importing richer external conceptual frameworks while maintaining stronger explicit rule-checking. The v1.8.0 specification update successfully eliminated the previously observed `=` ambiguity loop, while higher-temperature runs surfaced a new underspecified constraint around `|`, suggesting that warm generative use acts as an ongoing stress test for the grammar. These findings support a distinction between substrate-mode and tool-mode GPSL engagement and suggest that temperature modulates not only diversity of output but the depth and manner of grammar internalization.
