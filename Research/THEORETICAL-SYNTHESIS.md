# Pod Dispatch — Round 20: Trajectory–Field Coupling and Final Model

## Summary

Round 20 consolidated the March 17–18 findings into a final theoretical model. The central question was whether stability in GPSL depends only on context, or also on the path through the system.

The result was a progressive refinement:

1. rule-based view rejected
2. failure-boundary view adopted
3. dynamic stability boundary recognized
4. relational stability established
5. probabilistic stability added
6. trajectory dependence identified
7. trajectory–field coupling confirmed as final model

## Final Statement

GPSL is a coupled dynamical system in which symbolic trajectories are shaped by, and in turn reshape, a probabilistic stability field under constraint.

## Why this matters

This reframes GPSL from:
- a symbolic language
- a notation system
- a grammar experiment

into:
- an empirical model of symbolic reasoning under constraint

## Key implication

The behavior of symbolic systems is not adequately described by static rules alone. It must be modeled as dynamic interaction between:
- attractors
- constraints
- trajectories
- failure boundaries
- architecture-dependent priors

The conceptual model stabilized. Further work should focus on:
- validation across more models
