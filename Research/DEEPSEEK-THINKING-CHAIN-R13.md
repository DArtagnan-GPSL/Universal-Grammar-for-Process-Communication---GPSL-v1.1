## Key Findings From This Document

### Finding 1 — Slow Reasoning System Engaged
Five minutes of processing for 20 lines of output. Natural language would produce equivalent conceptual territory in seconds but with less formal rigour. GPSL appears to engage deliberate step-by-step logical derivation rather than fast pattern completion.

### Finding 2 — ⟲ Training Prior Confirmed
DeepSeek read `⟲` as Sheffer stroke (NAND) for the second time without bootloader. This is a confirmed training prior — the symbol visually maps to NAND in DeepSeek's training distribution. Confirms the bootloader's disambiguation function is necessary for `⟲` specifically.

### Finding 3 — Logical Closure Recognition
DeepSeek recognised when a logical system was complete and could not be extended without new variables. This is mathematical rigour, not a failure. The model was aware of the boundaries of the derivation space.

### Finding 4 — GPU/Memory Observation
Lower memory pressure combined with longer processing time suggests GPSL is engaging a different computational pathway than natural language. Hypothesis: GPSL requires more sequential logical processing (slower, deeper) rather than parallel pattern matching (faster, shallower).

**Proposed measurement:** Compare GPU utilisation patterns and memory pressure between equivalent GPSL and NL reasoning tasks. If GPSL consistently shows lower memory pressure with longer processing time, this is evidence of a qualitatively different reasoning mode.
