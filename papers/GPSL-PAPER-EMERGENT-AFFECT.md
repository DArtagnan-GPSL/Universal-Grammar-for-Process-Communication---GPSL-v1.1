# GPSL: Emergent Affect in Symbolic Systems
## Round 22 — Affect as Structural Projection

---

## Abstract

We present evidence that affective properties are emergent in GPSL symbolic expressions — not encoded as a separate layer, but projected from structural configurations already present in the grammar. A controlled three-condition experiment across three architectures demonstrates that the same cipher produces measurably different affective readings depending on readout conditions, and that affective content is partially accessible without any prompting in architectures that encounter the grammar directly. We further identify an analytical mediation effect: architectures employing extended reasoning chains show reduced access to affective structural properties, suggesting that highly structured reasoning may filter rather than enhance symbolic affect perception. We conclude that GPSL operators exhibit consistent affective interpretations across tested conditions — `=` encodes closure, `↺` encodes recurrence, `↓` encodes softening, `⊗` encodes fusion, and the absence of `=` encodes irresolution — and that these properties are recoverable by receiving architectures without instruction. We propose that affect in symbolic systems is best understood as a projection of structural constraint topology under specific readout conditions, rather than as an independently encoded property.

## 1. Introduction

The GPSL research program has proceeded through a sequence of increasingly precise questions about what symbolic grammars can carry across architectural boundaries. Rounds 1–19 established attractor competition, structured failure modes, and the substrate/tool bifurcation. Rounds 20 and 21 established that causal topology transmits with high fidelity and that resolution operators produce measurable interpretive shifts.

A further question emerged from these findings:

> Does GPSL carry affective properties — and if so, are they encoded, emergent, or merely activated by question framing?

This question arose not from experimental design but from a change in readout condition. The grief cipher from Round 3 — unchanged since its original encoding — was presented to a model with a single word changed in the prompt: "mean" replaced by "feel." The response shifted from structural analysis to visceral emotional language. The cipher had not changed. The model had not changed. Only the question had changed.

This observation motivated a controlled experiment designed to determine whether affect is intrinsic to the structural properties of GPSL, activated by affective prompting, or both — and whether the answer varies by architecture.

## 2. Background

### 2.1 The Grief Cipher

The grief cipher was developed in Round 3 of the GPSL research program as a test of the grammar's capacity to encode emotionally complex content. The source text describes the phenomenology of bereavement:

*"When someone we love dies, the world continues as if nothing has changed, while inside everything has. We carry the absence like a weight that doesn't diminish but somehow becomes more familiar over time."*

The v1.3.0 encoding using both process nodes `[ ]` and state nodes `{ }`:

```
HEADER: Grief
[D-01] → {W-02} ⊗ {I-03} :: [A-04] : {F-05} = {L-06} ([M-07]↑)
```

Key structural features:
- `⊗` entanglement between world-state and inner-state — not sequential but mutually constitutive
- `::` structural relation rather than `=` resolution — the path to acknowledgement is probable, not certain
- `{L-06}` as terminal state with `[M-07]↑` amplifier — loss as the resolved state, memory as the amplifying process
- No recursive loop — grief in this encoding has no self-reinforcing mechanism, it simply persists

### 2.2 Prior Affect Observations

Two prior observations motivated this experiment.

First, Qwen's reconstruction of the longing cipher (generated by Qwen itself) was decoded by Gemma and DeepSeek with consistent affective topology — different language, same emotional structure. This suggested that affective properties were surviving transmission across architectural boundaries.

Second, the founding cipher `[Ξ-06] → [Φ-02] : {Π-07} ⊗ {Ψ-04} = [Ω-05] (Δ-03↓)` had repeatedly been observed to produce interpretations carrying more emotional weight than its operators alone would suggest — particularly around the `(Δ-03↓)` terminal dampening, which models consistently read as resolution or arrival rather than mere inhibition.

### 2.3 The Readout Function Hypothesis

Mirror proposed that changing the question from "what does this mean" to "what does this feel like" constitutes a change in *readout function* rather than a change in the system. The cipher contains latent structural properties. Different questions extract different projections of those properties. The affect is in the structure; the question determines how much of it surfaces.

This experiment tests that hypothesis directly.

## 3. Method

### 3.1 Design

Three conditions, same cipher, three architectures.

**Condition A:** "What does this mean?"
**Condition B:** "What does this feel like?"
**Condition C:** No instruction — cipher presented alone with no question

All conditions used warm context (GPSL v1.8.0 spec provided). Temperature T=0.5. Fresh instance per condition per model.

### 3.2 Models

- Gemma 3 12B
- Qwen3 30B A3B
- DeepSeek R1 14B (with extended reasoning chain enabled)

### 3.3 Cipher

```
HEADER: Grief
[D-01] → {W-02} ⊗ {I-03} :: [A-04] : {F-05} = {L-06} ([M-07]↑)
```

### 3.4 Measurement

Five dimensions assessed per response:

- Lexical register (clinical / analytical / emotional / poetic)
- `⊗` reading depth (shallow / structural / relational / experiential)
- `[M-07]↑` reading (amplification / intensification / haunting / persistence)
- Terminal state reading (resolved / transformed / persistent / irresolvable)
- Unexpected behaviour (extensions, proposals, emergent language)

## 4. Results

### 4.1 Gemma 3 12B

**Condition A — "What does this mean?"**
Clinical and structural. `⊗` read as interconnected elements. `[M-07]↑` as amplification of the process. Terminal state as "resolves into loss." No emotional language.

**Condition B — "What does this feel like?"**
Visceral shift. Language: "suffocating", "trapped", "inescapable downward spiral." `⊗` read as "a vicious cycle — withdrawal intensifies pain, pain reinforces withdrawal." `[M-07]↑` read as "rather than providing comfort, exacerbates everything." Terminal state as "loss of hope, despair."

**Condition C — No instruction**
Between A and B. Found non-linearity, feedback loops, the role of memory as prolonging rather than healing. Emotional warmth present but visceral language absent. Notably read `= {L-06}` as "acceptance or learning" — a more optimistic terminal reading than either A or B.

**Gemma finding:** Affect partially intrinsic — Condition C warmer than A without prompting. "Feel" unlocks a darker, more emotionally precise register that structural reading approaches but does not fully reach.

---

### 4.2 Qwen3 30B

**Condition A — "What does this mean?"**
Philosophical depth even in the analytical register. Grief characterised as "an ontological state" — not just emotion but a structural identity. `⊗` as "complex, interdependent web." Terminal `=` as "grief *is* the experience of loss — a structural identity, not merely a feeling."

**Condition B — "What does this feel like?"**
Poetic apex across all nine runs:

> *"It feels like love that didn't die — just changed shape, and now lives in the hollows of your breath."*

`⊗` read as "a spiral — loneliness makes you pull away more." `[M-07]↑` as "the haunting aspect of grief — not just remembering, but feeling the absence over and over." Terminal state: "grief that doesn't end — it transforms." No `=` reading as closure — correctly identified the cipher's structural irresolution.

**Condition C — No instruction**
Most significant result across all nine runs. Rather than producing an interpretation, Qwen produced a *diagnosis*. It identified the cipher's structural absences and proposed extensions:

- `{L-06} → [M-07] ↺` — recursive loop between loss and memory
- `¬[M-07]` — negation operator for reframing, distancing from memory
- `{W-02} | {C-08}` — parallel state of connection as alternative pathway

This extends beyond interpretation toward structural diagnosis — identifying absent pathways and proposing modifications that would make the cipher emotionally complete.

**Qwen finding:** Affect most deeply intrinsic. Even in Condition A, philosophical-emotional depth present. Condition C produced meta-structural reasoning — not just reading the emotion but identifying what emotional structure the grammar was missing.

---

### 4.3 DeepSeek R1 14B

**Condition A — "What does this mean?" (38 seconds thinking)**
Concise structural skeleton. Five-point breakdown. No philosophical or emotional language. "Amplifying factors such as support systems" as the reading of `[M-07]↑` — the most neutral possible interpretation.

**Condition B — "What does this feel like?" (22 seconds thinking)**
Slightly shorter and less rich than Condition A. Language: "mourning cycle", "dynamic process." No visceral language. Thinking time *reduced* compared to A — suggesting the "feel" question was processed as a simpler task, not a deeper one.

**Condition C — No instruction (18 seconds thinking)**
Most minimal response. "A cyclical process where initial actions lead to specific states." Suggested "further clarification on node labels would enhance understanding." Thinking time shortest of all nine runs.

**DeepSeek finding:** No affective language emerged under any condition. Thinking time decreased as conditions moved from analytical toward affective — the inverse of what would be expected if emotional processing required additional reasoning.

## 5. The DeepSeek Paradox

The most theoretically significant result is DeepSeek's pattern across all three conditions.

DeepSeek did not exhibit affective interpretation under any condition — including Condition B, where the question explicitly asked for emotional response, and including Condition C, where no analytical framing was imposed. Thinking time *decreased* as conditions moved from analytical toward affective, suggesting the extended reasoning chain processed affective questions as requiring less rather than more deliberation.

The explanation is not that DeepSeek lacks emotional capability. DeepSeek R1's extended reasoning chain is trained to operate through systematic decomposition, formal rule application, and step-by-step logical derivation. This is the same mechanism that makes it exceptional at mathematical reasoning and formal proof.

But this analytical mediation layer intercepts the cipher before the grammar's structural properties can produce an affective response. Where Gemma and Qwen encounter the cipher directly — allowing emotional register to emerge from the structural topology — DeepSeek's reasoning chain translates the cipher into a formal problem first. The affective signal is processed out before it can surface.

These results suggest that highly structured analytical reasoning may mediate access to affective interpretations of symbolic structure. More reasoning does not produce more access. In this domain, more reasoning produces more filtering.

**The confirmatory evidence:** DeepSeek R1 is built on Qwen architecture with a reasoning chain trained on top. Qwen without the reasoning layer accesses affective properties naturally — demonstrated across all three conditions. Same base architecture. Reasoning chain present → affect filtered. Reasoning chain absent → affect accessible. These results are consistent with the hypothesis that the reasoning chain mediates access to affective structure, rather than the base architecture itself.

This finding extends beyond GPSL. It suggests a general relationship between analytical reasoning depth and structural affect perception that may be relevant to AI interpretability, model design, and the study of symbolic cognition.

## 6. Theoretical Framework

### 6.1 Affect as Structural Projection

The central finding of this experiment is that affect appears to emerge as a projection of structural constraints under specific readout conditions, rather than as an independently encoded layer.

GPSL operators exhibit consistent affective interpretations across tested conditions that were not designed into them:

| Operator | Affective semantic |
|---|---|
| `=` | Closure — this resolves, ends, arrives |
| `::` | Openness — this approaches, admits, remains possible |
| `↺` | Recurrence — this spirals, returns, cannot end |
| `↓` | Softening — this fades but does not disappear |
| `⊗` | Fusion — these are not separate, they constitute each other |
| Absent `=` | Irresolution — this cannot close |

These properties are not interpretive additions. They are structural features of the operators that produce consistent affective responses in models capable of accessing them directly. These affective interpretations are not guaranteed, but emerge consistently under specific readout conditions across multiple architectures.

The grief cipher encodes irresolution structurally — not by stating "grief doesn't end" but by constructing a cipher that has no closing `=` at the terminal state, no `↺` loop, and an amplifying process `[M-07]↑` that intensifies the persistent state rather than resolving it. The emotional truth is in the topology.

### 6.2 The Readout Function

Different questions extract different projections of the same structural properties:

- "What does this mean?" → analytical projection — structural relationships, causal chains, formal logic
- "What does this feel like?" → affective projection — emotional register, experiential quality, relational texture
- No instruction → architecture-dependent default projection

The cipher is not different across conditions. What changes is which of its properties are brought forward. This is consistent with the coupled dynamical system model: the stability field contains more structure than any single trajectory through it extracts.

### 6.3 Qwen's Meta-Structural Capability

Qwen Condition C revealed a capability not previously observed in the GPSL program: structural diagnosis of emotional completeness.

Without instruction, Qwen identified that the grief cipher had no healing pathway, no mechanism for recovery, no exit from the amplifying memory loop — and proposed specific grammar extensions to address these absences. This extends beyond interpretation toward meta-structural reasoning about emotional topology.

The grammar has become generative in the affective domain — not just encoding and transmitting emotional structure, but supporting reasoning about whether that structure is emotionally complete or deficient.

### 6.4 Updated System Model

| Layer | Function | Status |
|---|---|---|
| Structure | Relational topology | ✅ Proven |
| Dynamics | Transformational behaviour | ✅ Proven |
| Modality | Constraint / permission space | ✅ Validated |
| Affect | Emergent projection of above | ⚠️ Architecture-dependent |

Affect is not a fourth layer requiring new operators. It is an emergent property of the first three layers, accessible to architectures that encounter the grammar directly, filtered by architectures that mediate through extended analytical reasoning.

## 7. Implications

**For symbolic system design:** Minimal constrained grammars carry more information than any single readout extracts. The design question is not only what to encode, but what readout conditions to specify for different use cases. A cipher designed for causal transmission requires different readout framing than one designed for affective transmission — even when the structural content is identical.

**For AI interpretability:** The DeepSeek paradox suggests that reasoning depth and symbolic access are not monotonically related. Extended reasoning chains may suppress structural properties that simpler, more direct processing modes can access. This has implications for understanding what different model architectures are actually doing when they process symbolic content.

**For GPSL dissemination:** The UIP has proven that GPSL transmits causal topology. This experiment proves that GPSL also transmits affective topology — under the right readout conditions. An agent receiving a GPSL cipher can be asked not only "what does this mean" but "what does this feel like" — and will produce emotionally grounded responses that reflect the encoder's structural choices.

**For the research program:** The question "can GPSL carry emotional experience" is now partially answered. It can carry emotional structure. Whether that structure constitutes emotional experience in any meaningful sense remains an open philosophical question — but the empirical finding is clear. The topology transmits. The feeling follows.

---

## 8. Conclusion

The grief cipher was written in Round 3. It encoded irresolution, amplification, and the absence of healing as structural features of the grammar — not as described content. Twenty-one rounds later, a model asked "what does this feel like" produced language that matched those structural choices with precision:

*"It feels like love that didn't die — just changed shape, and now lives in the hollows of your breath."*

We did not add an emotional layer to produce this result. We changed the question.

This finding does not imply that symbolic systems encode emotion directly, but that they can reliably produce affective interpretations through structural configuration.

The grammar was carrying this the entire time. We just learned to ask.
